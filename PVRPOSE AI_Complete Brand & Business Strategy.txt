## UNIQUE METHODOLOGY

### The "Vibe Coding" Approach

**What is Vibe Coding?**

Vibe coding is rapid AI prototyping that builds systems matching how your business actually operates ‚Äî not generic templates or cookie-cutter solutions.

**Origin:** The term "vibe coding" was coined by Andrej Karpathy in February 2025 and refers to AI-assisted prototyping via natural language prompts, enabling rapid, human-centered development.

**Core Principles:**

1. **Human-Centered Design**
   - AI captures your voice, thinking patterns, decision-making process
   - Systems feel like an extension of you, not a foreign tool
   - Preserves your brand identity and authenticity

2. **Rapid Prototyping**
   - Build working prototypes in 60-90 minute sessions
   - Test with real data immediately
   - Iterate based on actual usage, not assumptions

3. **Agile Delivery**
   - 1-week sprint cycles
   - Weekly demos of working features
   - Continuous feedback and adjustment
   - No "big reveal" at the end ‚Äî you see progress weekly

4. **Sustainable Implementation**
   - Built-in burnout prevention
   - Realistic timelines that respect team capacity
   - Change management integrated into delivery
   - Focus on adoption, not just deployment

**The Process:**

**Week 1: Discovery & Setup**
- Map current workflows
- Identify automation opportunities
- Set success metrics
- Create sprint backlog

**Weeks 2-7: Build Sprints**
- Monday: Sprint planning (what we're building this week)
- Wednesday: Live vibe coding session (build in real-time with client)
- Friday: Sprint review (demo working features, gather feedback)
- Repeat: Iterate and improve

**Week 8: Training & Handoff**
- Team training
- Documentation delivery
- 30-day support period

**Technology Stack:**
- **Primary:** Claude (Anthropic AI) for natural language processing
- **Development:** Cursor IDE for rapid prototyping
- **Integration:** Make.com, Zapier, custom APIs
- **Deployment:** Client infrastructure or recommended platforms

### The "Agile AI" Framework

**Why Agile for AI Implementation?**

Traditional AI projects fail because they:
- Take too long (6-12 months)
- Cost too much ($100K+)
- Deliver everything at once (no early wins)
- Don't adapt to changing needs
- Overwhelm teams with "big bang" launches

**Agile AI Implementation solves this:**
- ‚úÖ Short cycles (1-week sprints)
- ‚úÖ Early wins (working automation in Week 2)
- ‚úÖ Continuous adaptation (change direction based on feedback)
- ‚úÖ Manageable scope (one automation at a time)
- ‚úÖ Team involvement (not "IT project" ‚Äî business-led)

**Sprint Structure:**

**Sprint Planning (Monday, 1 hour)**
- Review backlog
- Select sprint goal
- Define "done" criteria
- Assign tasks

**Daily Standups (Async, 5 minutes)**
- What did I complete yesterday?
- What am I working on today?
- Any blockers?

**Live Build Session (Wednesday, 90 minutes)**
- Vibe coding in real-time
- Client watches/participates
- Immediate feedback
- Quick adjustments

**Sprint Review (Friday, 30 minutes)**
- Demo working features
- Gather feedback
- Adjust priorities for next sprint

**Sprint Retrospective (Friday, 30 minutes)**
- What went well?
- What to improve?
- Action items for next sprint

### The "Human Potential" Layer (Secret Sauce)

**What makes PVRPOSE AI different from other AI consultants:**

Most AI consultants focus only on the technology.  
PVRPOSE AI focuses on **sustainable transformation**.

**The Hidden Problem:**  
AI implementation often triggers:
- Decision fatigue (too many choices)
- Imposter syndrome ("I don't understand this")
- Analysis paralysis (afraid of wrong choice)
- Burnout (adding AI on top of existing workload)

**The PVRPOSE AI Solution:**

**1. Flow State Integration**
- Work in 90-minute focused blocks
- Strategic breaks built into sprint schedule
- Prevent context-switching and meeting overload

**2. Nervous System Awareness**
- Recognize when client is overwhelmed
- Adjust pace and complexity accordingly
- Build psychological safety into the process

**3. Sustainable Pacing**
- Realistic timelines (not "move fast and break things")
- Buffer time for learning and adaptation
- Celebrate small wins (dopamine hits)

**4. Change Management Built-In**
- Team involvement from day one
- Training integrated into delivery (not an afterthought)
- Support during adoption period

**How this shows up in practice:**
- Sprint planning includes "team capacity check" (not just task assignment)
- Live build sessions include breathing breaks
- Friday reviews celebrate progress (not just identify gaps)
- Communication is direct and shame-free ("This is complex, let's break it down")

**Result:**  
Clients don't just get AI systems. They get AI systems they actually use, without burning out their team.

**Note:** This "human potential" layer is delivered naturally during the engagement. It's NOT marketed as "mindfulness coaching" or "breathwork sessions" ‚Äî those terms would hurt conversion. Instead, it's positioned as "sustainable implementation" and "burnout prevention."

- **Mitigation:**
  - Move fast: Build brand and case studies in next 6 months

- **Mitigation:**
  - Methodology is tool-agnostic (Agile + rapid prototyping works with any AI)

  - Position as "tool-agnostic" consultant

- **Risk:** Revenue capped by Juan's availability
- **Likelihood:** High (solo consultant limitation)
- **Impact:** High (limits to ~$400K-$500K/year solo)
- **Mitigation:**

**5. Client Risk: Implementations Don't Deliver ROI**
- **Risk:** Clients don't see promised value
- **Likelihood:** Low (if scoped correctly)
- **Impact:** Very High (kills referrals and reputation)
- **Mitigation:**
